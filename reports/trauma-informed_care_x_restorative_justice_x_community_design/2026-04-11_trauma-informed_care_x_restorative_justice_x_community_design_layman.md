## Abstract

423 sources spanning Trauma-informed care × restorative justice × community design were mapped into a concept graph with 5,767 connections. The structural analysis found 20 cross-domain bridge concepts, 7 distinct research communities with 94% structural overlap between the two largest, convergence signals where independent researchers arrived at similar conclusions without citing each other. The intersection is more active than either field appears to know.

## How to Read This Report

DCFN maps the *structure* of published research — which papers cite which, how concepts cluster, and where fields share unexplored common ground. It does this by pulling sources from seven databases, encoding them into a concept graph, and measuring proximity.

Three things to keep in mind:

- **Connections are structural, not causal.** When this report says two fields converge, it means their citation patterns and vocabulary overlap — not that one causes the other.
- **The engine reads titles and abstracts, not full papers.** A paper whose main contribution lives deep in its methodology section may be underweighted.
- **"Untested foundation" means citation-stale, not debunked.** If a foundational paper is flagged, it means recent work has stopped actively re-citing it — not that its findings are wrong.

Everything below operates inside these boundaries.

## The Intersection

The intersection between trauma-informed care and restorative justice emerges from their shared recognition that past harm fundamentally shapes present behavior and relationships. Both fields draw extensively from the same foundational literature on trauma's neurobiological and social impacts, creating a densely overlapping citation network that spans psychology, criminology, and public health research. This structural convergence reflects overlapping methodologies that prioritize understanding individual experience within broader systems of harm and healing, positioning both approaches as responses to the same fundamental challenge of addressing damage while preventing its recurrence.
