## How to Read This Report

DCFN maps the *structure* of published research — which papers cite which, how concepts cluster, and where fields share unexplored common ground. It does this by pulling sources from seven databases, encoding them into a concept graph, and measuring proximity.

Three things to keep in mind:

- **Connections are structural, not causal.** When this report says two fields converge, it means their citation patterns and vocabulary overlap — not that one causes the other.
- **The engine reads titles and abstracts, not full papers.** A paper whose main contribution lives deep in its methodology section may be underweighted.
- **"Untested foundation" means citation-stale, not debunked.** If a foundational paper is flagged, it means recent work has stopped actively re-citing it — not that its findings are wrong.

Everything below operates inside these boundaries.
